ISS Facts

ITET Python for Engineers

1 Basic Information

  • Name: International Space Station (ISS)
  • Launched: First module launched in 1998
  • First crewed mission: November 2, 2000 (Expedition 1)
  • Orbital altitude: ~400 km (248 miles) above Earth
  • Speed: 28,000 km/h (17,500 mph)
  • Orbits Earth: About 16 times per day

2 Structure & Design

  • Length: ~73 meters (240 feet)
  • Width: ~109 meters (356 feet)
  • Mass: ~420,000 kg (925,000 pounds)
  • Pressurized modules: 16+
  • Solar arrays: Provide 110 kilowatts of power

3 International Collaboration

Partner agencies: NASA (USA), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), CSA (Canada)

4 Crew & Life on Board

  • Crew size: 3 to 10 astronauts and cosmonauts
  • Longest continuous human presence in space: Since 2000
  • Crew rotation: Soyuz or SpaceX Crew Dragon
  • Daily life: Experiments, exercise, maintenance, Earth observation

5 Scientific Purpose

  • Microgravity effects on the human body
  • Fluid dynamics, biology, materials science
  • Earth and space observation
  • Technology testing for future Mars missions

6 Transport

Supplies and crew delivered by: SpaceX Dragon / Crew Dragon, Cygnus, Soyuz / Progress (and previously Space Shuttle)

7 Fun Facts

  • Visible from Earth with the naked eye
  • Internal volume: ~388 m³ (like a Boeing 747)
  • Over 260 people from 20+ countries have visited
  • Astronauts exercise about 2 hours daily
